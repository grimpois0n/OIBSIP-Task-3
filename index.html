Biography

Bjarne Stroustrup, born on December 30, 1950, in Aarhus, Denmark, is a renowned computer scientist best known as the creator of the C++ programming language. After obtaining his Master's degree in mathematics and computer science, Stroustrup joined Bell Labs in 1979, where he developed C++ as an extension of the C programming language, introducing object-oriented programming concepts. C++ became widely adopted, revolutionizing software development. Stroustrup has held academic positions, authored influential books on C++, and received awards for his contributions. His enduring impact on programming and computer science continues through teaching, research, and active participation in the programming community.
